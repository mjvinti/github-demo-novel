==========
CHARACTERS
==========

Jay Catsby
- Role: The tragic protagonist and a mysterious self-made millionaire.
- Background: Born James Gatz to poor Midwestern farmers, he reinvented himself as Jay Catsby and amassed a fortune through illegal bootlegging to win back his lost love, Daisy.
- Personality: Optimistic, fiercely devoted, and trapped by the illusion of the past.
- Significance: He represents the corrupted American Dream and the tragic consequences of valuing materialism and idealism over reality.

Nick Carraway
- Role: The first-person narrator and Daisy's cousin.
- Background: A young Midwesterner who moves to New York to work in the bond business. He rents a modest home in West Egg next to Catsby’s extravagant mansion.
- Personality: Observant, quiet, and typically level-headed, acting as a trusted confidant for the other characters.
- Significance: He serves as the moral compass of the story, acting as a bridge between the reckless "new rich" of West Egg and the established "old money" of East Egg.

Daisy Buchanan
- Role: The beautiful socialite and the center of Catsby's obsession.
- Background: Raised in wealth and privilege, she dated Catsby during WWI but ultimately chose security, marrying Tom Buchanan for his social status.
- Personality: Charming, fragile, but ultimately shallow and deeply self-centered.
- Significance: She represents the allure of the elite upper class, but her inability to take responsibility for her actions—like accidentally killing Tom's mistress while driving—ultimately drives the tragic finale.

Thomas (Tom) Buchanan
- Role: Daisy's husband and Catsby's main rival.
- Background: A wealthy, arrogant Yale graduate from an old-money family, he moves his family from Chicago to East Egg.
- Personality: Racist, sexist, aggressive, and a hypocrite who has an ongoing affair with Myrtle Wilson but acts outraged by his wife's infidelity.
- Significance: He embodies the oppressive, entitled nature of the "old money" elite who use their wealth to manipulate others and escape the consequences of their actions
