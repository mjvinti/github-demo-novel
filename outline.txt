Chapter 1
- We meet Nick, Tom, Daisy and Jordan
- Narrator Nick Carraway introduces himself, explaining his Midwestern roots, Yale education, and move to New York in 1922 to learn the bond business. He rents a modest home in West Egg—a Long Island community for the newly wealthy. He visits his cousin Daisy and her husband, Tom Buchanan, in neighboring East Egg, which is populated by the "old money" elite. There, Nick meets professional golfer Jordan Baker, and becomes aware of Tom’s infidelity. Returning home, Nick spots his mysterious neighbor, Jay Catsby, standing on his lawn, gazing longingly at a green light across the water.
- Key Events & Observations
  - The Narrator’s Background: Nick comes from a prominent, well-off Midwestern family and is a World War I veteran. He prides himself on being an open-minded, non-judgmental observer—a trait that makes him a perfect confidant.
  - New Money vs. Old Money: Chapter 1 heavily establishes the setting as SparkNotes explains.
    - West Egg: Represents the flashy, "new money" class. This is where Nick rents a small bungalow right next to Catsby’s extravagant mansion.
    - East Egg: Represents the established, aristocratic "old money" class. This is where Nick's cousin Daisy and her hulking, arrogant ex-Yale classmate husband, Tom Buchanan, live in a lavish estate.
  - Dinner at the Buchanans: Nick reconnects with Daisy and is introduced to her friend, Jordan Baker. During dinner, Tom dominates the conversation by expressing racist and pseudo-scientific beliefs he read about in a book.
  - The Affair is Revealed: The dinner is interrupted by phone calls from Tom’s mistress in New York. While Tom is out of the room, Jordan informs Nick about the affair, and Daisy confesses to Nick that she is deeply unhappy and cynical about her life.
  - The Green Light: After the awkward, tense evening, Nick returns to West Egg. He steps outside and catches his first glimpse of his neighbor, Jay Catsby. Catsby is standing alone in the dark, trembling and reaching out toward a mysterious, solitary green light shining across the water.

  Chapter 2
  - Nick goes home and sees Catsby for the first time
  - Chapter 2 shifts from the glamorous world of the wealthy to the desolate "Valley of Ashes."
  - Tom Buchanan takes Nick Carraway there to meet his loud, vibrant mistress, Myrtle Wilson.
  - They travel to a Manhattan apartment where an alcohol-fueled, rowdy party ends abruptly when Tom violently breaks Myrtle’s nose.
